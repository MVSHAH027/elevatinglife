Renunciation of Attachment and Performance of Duty

The Process of Letting Go of Attachment

It is difficult to give up worldly attachments like attachment to a son. Intense renunciation and the effort to attain restraint weaken this attachment. Although this process is painful, its ultimate result is supremely blissful.

"Painful process, blissful result."

The True Nature of the World: A Dream-like Vision

The Symptom of Right Vision (Samyakdrishti)

The soul with right vision sees all events of home, business, society, etc., as similar to events in a 'dream'. These events include dialogue, happiness-sorrow, attachment-aversion, necessity, duty, etc., but they have no real meaning or effect.

"Just as everything seems illusory after waking from a dream, so does this world appear to the enlightened one."

Cultivating this vision and passing through worldly dealings appropriately, in a way that does not slander Dharma, is the essence. Worldly illusion and attachment are harmful to both self and others and cause wandering in lower states of existence.

'I' (Aham) and 'Mine' (Mam): Towards True Identity

üëç Commendable 'I' and 'Mine' (Uplifting)

Commendable 'I': In the form of self-awareness (awareness of one's pure soul-nature).

Commendable 'Mine': In the form of attachment to the soul's qualities (attachment to one's own qualities like knowledge, vision, character).

This uplifts the soul, causes progress.

üëé Uncommendable 'I' and 'Mine' (Degrading)

Uncommendable 'I': The sensation of 'I' in the body (considering the body as the soul).

Uncommendable 'Mine': Attachment to relatives, property, etc. (attachment to external objects and relationships).

This drowns the soul, deludes it.

The Path of Spiritual Progress

The essence of Jinashasana (Jain teachings) is that the soul should abandon the uncommendable 'I-mine' and adopt the commendable 'I-mine'. The worldly environment constantly nourishes uncommendable feelings, while Jinashasana nourishes commendable feelings and leads the soul towards the ultimate state.

Jinashasana: A Divine System

Jinashasana has created such a wonderful system that constantly nourishes the commendable 'I-mine', keeps the soul conscious, and leads to the attainment of the ultimate state along with a tradition of good destiny. This system is fully equipped in every way; we only have to benefit from it and become fulfilled.
